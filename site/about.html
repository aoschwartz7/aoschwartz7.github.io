I started learning to code while I was working for a small Biotech startup in the Boston area, motivated by the limitations I encountered in Excel’s data processing functions. I enrolled in Codecademy’s Python course and I started to really enjoy the logic it took to get tiny programs to run. I tried writing some small scripts for analysis but didn't apply them then—our experiments and data formats were always changing. So I kept learning at home, enrolling in more courses (one on SQL, and another on data analysis). At the end of the company’s two year funding cap, we were ultimately denied further funding and we were all laid off. It was at this point that I decided to take a risk and jump into what I’d been teaching myself at home for the past year.

Since then, I've written programs with Python and C, and built a web application with the Flask framework. Striving now to build useful, creative programs hasn’t just become a fun challenge; it’s showing me how programming can apply to virtually every field—areas spanning well beyond biology research and drug discovery. Now I'm searching for an opportunity where I can continue to develop these skills.
